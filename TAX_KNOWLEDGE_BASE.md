## 🎯 COMPLETE RELIEF BREAKDOWN (2024)

### 1. PERSONAL RELIEFS

#### A. Self Relief
- **Amount**: RM 9,000 (automatic for all)
- **Who qualifies**: Every Malaysian taxpayer earning above minimum threshold
- **Conditions**: 
  - Must be a Malaysian resident for tax purposes
  - Applies to both employed and self-employed
- **Documentation**: None needed (automatic)
- **Source**: Schedule 3, Income Tax Act 1967

#### B. Spouse Relief
- **Amount**: RM 9,000 (one spouse only per year)
- **Who qualifies**: Married individuals, widows/widowers
- **Key points**:
  - Only ONE spouse can claim (usually higher earner)
  - Cannot claim for same person twice
  - Applies even if spouse has no income
  - Includes legally married same-sex partners (as per recent updates)
- **Documentation**: Marriage certificate
- **Source**: Section 49, Income Tax Act 1967

#### C. Children Relief
- **Amount**: RM 3,000 per child (max 4 children)
- **Who qualifies**: Parents with dependent children
- **Eligible children**:
  - Unmarried children under age 18
  - Under age 25 if in full-time education
  - Unmarried regardless of age if disabled
  - Adopted children (with legal documents)
- **Key limits**:
  - Cannot exceed 4 children per year
  - Extra children not claimable
  - Both parents cannot claim same child (choose highest earner)
- **Documentation**: Birth certificates, school enrollment proof
- **Source**: Section 50, Income Tax Act 1967

#### D. Dependent Parents/In-laws Relief
- **Amount**: RM 2,000 per parent (max 2 parents)
- **Who qualifies**: Those supporting elderly parents
- **Age requirement**: Parent/in-law must be 60 years or older
- **Income requirement**: Parent's annual income must not exceed RM4,000
- **Key points**:
  - Must be supporting them financially
  - Can claim up to 2 parents
  - Only if parent doesn't earn above threshold
  - Includes both biological and in-laws
- **Documentation**: Birth certificate, proof of support, income proof
- **Source**: Section 51A, Income Tax Act 1967

### 2. MEDICAL & HEALTH RELIEFS

#### A. Medical Expenses Relief
- **Amount**: RM 5,000 per year
- **Qualifying expenses**:
  - Outpatient medical checkups
  - Hospitalization costs (not covered by insurance)
  - Dental treatment (filling, cleaning, extraction, dentures)
  - Optical treatment (glasses, contact lenses, treatment)
  - Medical examination fees
  - Prescribed medication
  - Medical diagnostic tests
  - Physiotherapy
  - Vaccination
- **NOT qualifying**:
  - Cosmetic surgery
  - Maternity cases (covered under wife's relief)
  - Fertility treatment
  - Preventive medications not on prescription
- **Key limits**:
  - RM 5,000 annual limit
  - Include only actual out-of-pocket costs
  - Insurance reimbursements not claimable
  - Family members' medical costs NOT claimable separately
- **Documentation**: Original receipts, medical reports, invoices
- **Source**: Section 51, Income Tax Act 1967

#### B. Life Insurance Relief
- **Amount**: RM 8,000 combined with medical insurance
- **Qualifying premiums**:
  - Life insurance policies (term, whole life)
  - Medical/health insurance premiums
  - Accident insurance
  - Critical illness insurance
  - Income protection insurance
- **NOT qualifying**:
  - Investment-linked insurance (separate treatment)
  - General insurance (car, home, travel)
  - Life insurance from employer (automatic deduction)
- **Key points**:
  - RM 8,000 is COMBINED limit for life + medical insurance
  - Premiums must be from your own pocket
  - Family members' insurance NOT claimable
  - Group insurance policies qualify
- **Documentation**: Insurance premium receipts/statements
- **Source**: Section 51, Income Tax Act 1967 (Schedule 3)

### 3. EDUCATION & TRAINING RELIEFS

#### A. Education Fees Relief
- **Amount**: RM 6,000 per year
- **Qualifying education**:
  - University/college tuition fees
  - Diploma courses (accredited institutions)
  - Professional courses (accounting, law, engineering)
  - Distance learning programs (accredited)
  - Pre-university courses (A-Levels, IB, Matriculation)
- **NOT qualifying**:
  - Primary/secondary school fees (covered elsewhere)
  - Tuition classes (not recognized institutions)
  - Online hobby courses
  - Non-accredited programs
- **Key points**:
  - Your own education only (not children's)
  - Full-time or part-time qualifies
  - Can be claimed by self-employed too
  - Year of first enrollment counts
- **Documentation**: Tuition receipts, admission letters, course details
- **Source**: Section 80, Income Tax Act 1967

#### B. SSPN/PTPTN Relief
- **Amount**: RM 8,000 per year
- **SSPN (Skim Simpanan Pendidikan)**: Education savings scheme
  - Contributions up to RM 6,000/year qualify
  - For children's education
  - Can claim even for future payments made
- **PTPTN (Perbadanan Tabung Pendidikan Tinggi Nasional)**: Student loan
  - Principal repayments claimable
  - Interest payments NOT separately claimable
  - Monthly payments accumulate to RM 8,000/year
  - Can claim for children's PTPTN too
- **Key points**:
  - RM 8,000 annual limit
  - Different from education fees relief
  - Can claim SSPN contributions even if child not in school yet
  - PTPTN repayments from age 18 onward
- **Documentation**: SSPN statements, PTPTN payment receipts
- **Source**: Section 82, Income Tax Act 1967

### 4. HOUSING LOAN RELIEFS

#### A. Residential Property Housing Loan Interest
- **Amount**: RM 6,000 per year (first 10 years ONLY)
- **Qualifying loans**:
  - Housing loan for residential property
  - Primary residence only
  - Interest portion only (principal NOT claimable)
- **Eligibility**:
  - Own the property
  - Occupy as primary residence
  - Valid only for FIRST 10 years of loan
  - After 10 years, relief ceases
- **NOT qualifying**:
  - Commercial/investment properties
  - Second homes/vacation homes
  - Loans not from financial institutions
  - Refinanced loans (only original 10-year period counts)
  - Land only (must be developed property)
- **Key calculation**:
  - Extract interest portion from loan statement
  - Claim up to RM 6,000/year
  - If interest < RM 6,000, claim actual amount
- **Crucial point**: After 10 years, you CANNOT claim anymore
- **Documentation**: Loan agreement, bank statements showing interest, property ownership docs
- **Source**: Section 61, Income Tax Act 1967

#### B. Stamp Duty on Housing Loan (One-time)
- **Amount**: 100% stamp duty exemption
- **Applies to**: First purchase of residential property
- **Conditions**:
  - First home purchase only
  - Property price RM 500,000 or less
  - Stamp duty NOT payable (100% exemption)
- **Note**: This is automatic, no claiming needed

### 5. LIFESTYLE & PERSONAL DEVELOPMENT RELIEFS

#### A. Lifestyle Expenses Relief
- **Amount**: RM 1,000 per year
- **Qualifying expenses**:
  - Books and educational materials
  - Internet subscription (personal use)
  - Computer/laptop (for work/study)
  - Sportswear and sports equipment
  - Gym membership
  - Online courses and training materials
  - e-book subscriptions
  - Work-related magazines/journals
  - Phone subscription (work-related)
  - Software subscriptions (work-related)
- **Key conditions**:
  - Must be for personal/professional development
  - Computer only if primary use is work (not gaming)
  - Sports equipment for active participation
  - Gym must be recognized facility
  - Keep receipts as evidence
- **NOT qualifying**:
  - Entertainment (movies, music streaming)
  - Luxury items (designer clothes)
  - Vehicles
  - Furniture
  - Alcoholic beverages
  - Tobacco products
- **RM 1,000 covers all**: This is TOTAL limit for ALL lifestyle expenses
- **Documentation**: Receipts, invoices, proof of payment
- **Source**: Section 80A, Income Tax Act 1967 (added 2008)

---

### 6. SPECIAL RELIEFS

#### A. Disabled Person Relief
- **Amount**: RM 6,000
- **Who qualifies**: 
  - Yourself if disabled
  - Depending parents if disabled
  - Depending siblings if disabled
- **Definition of disabled**:
  - Recognized by Malaysian medical professionals
  - Registered with disability authorities
  - Permanent or temporary disability
- **Documentation**: Medical certification, disability ID
- **Source**: Section 48, Income Tax Act 1967

#### B. Dependent Sister/Brother Relief
- **Amount**: RM 2,000 per unmarried sibling
- **Conditions**:
  - Sibling must be unmarried
  - Earning less than RM4,000/year
  - You're supporting them financially
  - Max 2 siblings
- **Documentation**: Birth certificate, proof of support, income proof

#### C. Industrial Building Allowance (IBA)
- **For self-employed/business**: 
  - 10% annual capital allowance on factory buildings
  - Spread over 10 years
- **Not applicable to**: Salaried employees

## 📋 FILING PROCEDURES & DEADLINES

### Annual Filing Timeline
```
January - February
├─ Tax year ends (31 Dec)
└─ Gather all receipts and documents

March - April
├─ LHDN opens e-Filing system
├─ Receive EA form from employer (by 31 March)
└─ Create MyAccount if first time

May - June
├─ File your return online (https://efiling.hasil.gov.my)
├─ Review relief amounts carefully
├─ Keep backup of submission
└─ DEADLINE: 30 June

July - September
├─ LHDN processes returns
└─ Refunds credited to bank account (6-8 weeks)

October - December
└─ Plan for next year
```

### Important Deadlines
- **EA Form Deadline**: 31 March (employer must send)
- **e-Filing Deadline**: 30 June (taxpayer must file)
- **PCB Collection**: Usually until 30 April (last payroll month)
- **Refund Processing**: 6-8 weeks after filing

### Late Filing Penalties
- 1 month late: RM 50 penalty + 10% interest
- 3 months late: RM 100 penalty + 15% interest  
- 6 months late: RM 300 penalty + 20% interest
- Prosecution possible if > 6 months

## ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Mistake 1: Claiming Past Relief Limits
- **Example**: Claiming RM 2,000 medical (limit is RM 5,000) ✓ OK
- **Example**: Claiming RM 6,000 medical (limit is RM 5,000) ✗ WRONG
- **Result**: LHDN may deny, audit, or request adjustment

### Mistake 2: Double-Claiming
- **Example**: Claiming same insurance as life insurance AND medical relief ✗ WRONG
- **Solution**: Choose highest category and claim only once

### Mistake 3: Claiming After 10 Years (Housing)
- **Example**: Claiming housing interest after 10 years ✗ WRONG
- **Check**: Only first 10 years of loan qualify
- **Solution**: Keep track of start date

### Mistake 4: Claiming Children Over Limit
- **Example**: Claiming 5 children (limit is 4) ✗ WRONG
- **Solution**: Choose highest expense children, max 4

### Mistake 5: Claiming Without Receipts
- **Example**: Claiming RM 500 medical without receipt ✗ RISKY
- **LHDN may**: Deny, request proof, audit
- **Solution**: Always keep original receipts

### Mistake 6: Incorrect PCB Amount
- **Example**: Entering estimated PCB instead of actual ✗ WRONG
- **Solution**: Add up all 12 monthly payslip PCB amounts

### Mistake 7: Claiming in Wrong Year
- **Example**: Claiming 2023 expenses in 2024 return ✗ WRONG
- **Solution**: Claim expenses in year incurred
