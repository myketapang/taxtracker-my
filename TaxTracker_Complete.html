Pre-Filing Checklist

Get ready to file with confidence

☐

1. Received EA Form from employer

Your employer should send this by 31 March. It shows your gross salary and PCB paid.

☐

2. Gathered all original receipts

Collect receipts for insurance, medical, education, and other expenses. Keep for 7 years.

☐

3. Recorded all expenses in Tracker

Use the Tracker tool to log all qualifying expenses with dates and amounts.

☐

4. Calculated reliefs using Estimator

Run the Estimator to see your estimated refund based on reliefs claimed.

☐

5. Total PCB calculated from payslips

Add up all PCB amounts from your 12 monthly payslips. This is what you've already paid.

☐

6. Reviewed all amounts

Double-check that all relief amounts are correct before submitting to LHDN.

☐

7. Ready to file

Go to https://efiling.hasil.gov.my and submit your self-assessment return.

📅 Filing Timeline

Jan - Feb

Year ends, gather receipts

Mar - Apr

LHDN opens e-Filing, receive EA form

May - Jun

File your return (Deadline: 30 June)

Jul - Sep

Refunds processed to bank account

📋 Important Notes

  • ✓ Filing Deadline: 30 June each year
  • ✓ Keep Records: Retain all receipts for 7 years
  • ✓ LHDN Portal: https://efiling.hasil.gov.my
  • ✓ Refund Processing: Usually 6-8 weeks
  • ✓ Amendments: File amended return if errors found

FAQ & Guides

Answers to common questions about Malaysian tax reliefs

Frequently Asked Questions

This app helps Malaysian salaried taxpayers identify reliefs they can claim and track their eligible expenses throughout the year. It provides estimates to help you understand potential refunds.
This app is designed for salaried individuals in Malaysia with basic tax situations. If you have business income, rental income, or complex deductions, please consult a professional accountant.
Tax reliefs are amounts you can deduct from your gross income to reduce your taxable income. Common reliefs include spouse relief, children relief, insurance premiums, medical expenses, and education fees.
The estimate is based on 2024 tax bands and standard assumptions. Actual tax liability depends on your exact circumstances. Always verify with LHDN e-Filing before submission.
Keep original receipts and supporting documents for all claimed expenses. LHDN may request these during an audit. Keep records for at least 7 years.
The filing deadline for salaried individuals in Malaysia is typically June 30 each year. You will receive notice from LHDN when filing opens (usually around April).
Log into the LHDN e-Filing portal (https://efiling.hasil.gov.my) with your MyAccount credentials. You can manually enter the relief amounts this app calculates for you.

Malaysian Tax Reliefs Overview

👤 Personal Reliefs

  • Self Relief: RM 9,000 (Automatic)
  • Spouse Relief: RM 9,000 (If married)
  • Children: RM 3,000 per child
  • Parents: RM 2,000 each (Age 60+)

🏥 Health & Insurance

  • Medical: RM 5,000
  • Insurance: RM 8,000
  • Checkups, dental, optical, treatment

📚 Education & Savings

  • Education: RM 6,000
  • SSPN/PTPTN: RM 8,000
  • University, college, education savings

🏠 Housing & Lifestyle

  • Housing Interest: RM 6,000
  • Lifestyle: RM 1,000
  • Books, internet, sports, gym
